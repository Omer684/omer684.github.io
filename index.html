About

Scientist in training,
engineer in practice

I'm Muhammad Omer, a BS Data Science student at PAF-IAST (graduating 2028) from Haripur, Pakistan. I don't wait until graduation — I build, experiment, and ship real systems that solve real problems.

On the analytics side, I've built end-to-end SQL analysis projects covering advanced window functions (LAG, RANK, NTILE), cohort retention analysis, RFM customer segmentation, and attrition modeling — all on real, messy public datasets of 100K+ rows.

My flagship ML work is an end-to-end License Plate Recognition pipeline using YOLOv8, OpenCV, and EasyOCR — optimized to run at ~0.6s/frame on CPU, achieving a 15–20× inference speedup over baseline through async threading and lazy model loading.

I've also built a Fleet Intelligence Platform — a logistics control-tower combining a Random Forest demand forecaster (R² = 0.94) with a SciPy linear-programming optimizer that reallocates idle fleet from surplus to deficit zones.

Beyond building, I serve as Director of Research & Innovation at Grow Pakistan Innovation Network, and I've completed data science job simulation programs through Deloitte, PM Accelerator, Simplilearn, and HackerRank. Always building.
