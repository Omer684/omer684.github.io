About

Scientist in training,
engineer in practice

I'm Muhammad Omer, a BS Data Science student at PAF-IAST (graduating 2028) from Haripur, Pakistan. I don't wait until graduation — I build, experiment, and ship real systems that solve real problems.

My flagship work is an end-to-end License Plate Recognition pipeline using YOLOv8, OpenCV, and EasyOCR — optimized to run at ~0.6s/frame on CPU, achieving a 15–20× inference speedup over baseline through async threading, lazy model loading, and resolution capping.

On the analytics side, my Global Suicide Rate Analysis project initially returned an almost too-good-to-be-true R² = 0.997 — until I traced it to a data-leakage bug (a feature derived directly from the target). I re-engineered the pipeline and landed on a defensible R² = 0.51 with Random Forest. I'd rather ship an honest number than an inflated one.

I've also built a Fleet Intelligence Platform — a logistics control-tower combining a Random Forest demand forecaster (R² = 0.94) with a SciPy linear-programming optimizer that reallocates idle fleet from surplus to deficit zones, deployed live via Streamlit. Beyond that: production-grade full-stack apps, network monitoring tools in pure Bash, and complete e-commerce platforms.

Certified by Deloitte, PM Accelerator, Simplilearn, and HackerRank. Interned at two data science organizations. Member of ML research groups. Always building.
